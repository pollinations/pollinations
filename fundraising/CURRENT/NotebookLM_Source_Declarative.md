# Pollinations.AI — Pitch Deck Slide Descriptions

## Slide 1: Title + Traction
Open with company name, tagline ("The Creator Economy for AI Experiences"), seed round. Immediately show proof: 3M monthly active end users, 500+ new creators/day, 500+ live apps across ~200 countries, 2.4M daily API requests, $0 marketing spend — all organic.

## Slide 2: Problem → Solution
The vibe coding era: anyone can build an AI app in an afternoon, but success punishes creators — viral app means thousands in compute bills. Users won't pay $10/month for a calorie tracker. Our solution: Compute + Distribution + Payouts in one platform. 38+ models, 14 providers, one API, zero infrastructure needed. Unified Pollen wallet across every app. 1 Pollen = $1.

## Slide 3: The Engine — Grant-to-Earn
Visualize the loop: GRANT (compute credits) → BUILD (creator ships app) → EARN (end users pay with Pollen) → SPLIT (creator gets paid, we take a cut). We subsidize creators to bootstrap supply — the grant is customer acquisition cost, not a giveaway. Like electricity: we power the apps, users pay for the electricity, creators share the revenue.

## Slide 4: Why Now + The YouTube of AI
Three converging forces: cost collapse (models cheap enough for grants), vibe coding (non-tech creators exploding supply), distribution gap (value shifting from models to apps). The parallel: YouTube didn't make videos — hosted them and paid creators → video explosion. Pollinations doesn't make apps — hosts them and pays creators → AI app explosion. First platform combining Compute, Distribution, and Payouts.

## Slide 5: Economics + Competitive Position
Users buy Pollen, creators earn a share, we take a cut. Pay-per-use, no subscriptions. Flywheel: more apps → more Pollen purchases → more creator revenue → more creators. ~$6K MRR today, revenue share activates Q2 2026. Three-layer stack: suppliers (HuggingFace, Replicate, Fal.AI) host models, aggregators (OpenRouter, Vercel) sell to devs, Pollinations sits on top with distribution + monetization. They charge developers — we subsidize creators and earn when their users pay.

## Slide 6: Defensibility + Team
Two moats: (1) two-sided network — creators go where users are, users go where apps are. (2) Community as engineering — open source contributors ship production code, lowering CAPEX. 17K+ Discord, 4K+ GitHub stars. Team: Thomas Haferlach (CEO) — AI & CS Edinburgh, Amazon, published researcher, 15+ years AI/creative communities. Elliot Fouchy (COO) — ops, finance, cloud partnerships (ByteDance, Azure), payment infra across 195 countries. Second-time founders, backed by Antler Berlin.

## Slide 7: Market + Roadmap
~50M potential creators across app stores, Roblox, vibe coding. Today: 1,000 apps, 3M users. Target: 100K–1M apps, 300M–3B users. 1 app ≈ 3,000 users. Three layers built in order: Infrastructure (DONE) — one API, all models. Economy (NOW) — Pollen credits, rev-share, payments. Discovery (NEXT) — marketplace, curation, cross-app identity.

## Slide 8: The Ask
€2.5M Seed Round. 50% Compute & Infrastructure, 30% Engineering, 20% Community & Growth. 18-month outcomes: 23K+ paying users, revenue share live, operationally cash flow positive.
