# Pollinations.AI — Flywheel Deck (8 slides)

Centers on the self-reinforcing loop: every element feeds the next. Show the machine.

## Slide 1: Cover + The Number
Company name, "The Creator Economy for AI Experiences", seed round. One stat: 3M users, 500+ apps, $0 marketing. This is what an organic flywheel looks like.

## Slide 2: The Flywheel
Visualize: Free API → creators build apps → apps attract users → users buy Pollen → revenue funds more grants → more creators join. Each turn of the wheel accelerates the next. This is the core of the business — everything else supports it.

## Slide 3: Why It Spins
Three tailwinds: AI costs collapsing (grants are cheap), vibe coding (millions of new creators), distribution gap (no one owns the app layer yet). The flywheel only works now because all three forces converge.

## Slide 4: The Product
One API, 38+ models, 14 providers. Zero backend needed. Pollen wallet — users pay per use (1 Pollen = $1) across every app. We grant compute (our CAC), users pay, creators earn. Show how an app gets built and monetized.

## Slide 5: Proof
500+ apps live in ~200 countries, 500+ new creators daily, 2.4M API calls/day. All organic. Roblox game example: 51M visits, $12K/month. ~$6K MRR at cost. Revenue share Q2 2026. Open source community as engineering multiplier — 17K Discord, 4K GitHub stars.

## Slide 6: Moats
Each turn of the flywheel deepens the moat. Two-sided network: hard to bootstrap, harder to leave. Apps are open source — they fork and multiply, seeding more supply. Community ships code, reducing our cost structure. Competitors (Fal, OpenRouter, Replicate) charge devs — they can't replicate the user-pays model without rebuilding everything.
