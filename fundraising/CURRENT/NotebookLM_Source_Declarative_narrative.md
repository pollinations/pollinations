# Pollinations.AI — Narrative Deck (8 slides)

Story arc: sets the scene, introduces tension, reveals the insight, shows proof.

## Slide 1: Cover + The Moment
Company name, "The Creator Economy for AI Experiences", seed round. Set the scene: teenagers are skipping school to vibe code. Anyone can build an AI app in an afternoon. A new creator class is emerging — and they need a platform.

## Slide 2: The Trap
Success punishes creators. Build an app, it goes viral, you owe thousands in compute. Every platform charges the developer. Users won't pay $10/month for a calorie tracker. Millions of new creators, no way to survive their own success.

## Slide 3: The Insight — YouTube of AI
YouTube didn't make videos — hosted them, paid creators, got the video explosion. We don't make apps — we host them, pay creators, get the AI app explosion. First platform combining compute + distribution + payouts. We don't compete with creators. We are the stage.

## Slide 4: How It Works
One API (38+ models, 14 providers), zero infrastructure needed. Users pay with Pollen (1 Pollen = $1) across all apps — one wallet, no subscriptions. We subsidize creators with compute grants (our CAC), users pay for usage, creators earn, we take a cut. Show the product.

## Slide 5: Proof It's Working
3M MAU, 500+ live apps, 500+ new creators/day, 2.4M daily API calls — all organic, $0 marketing. ~$6K MRR at cost pricing. Example: one creator-built Roblox game, 51M visits, $12K/month user spend. Revenue share activates Q2 2026.

## Slide 6: Market + Why Now
~50M potential creators. Models commoditizing — value shifting to distribution. Three forces: cost collapse enables grants, vibe coding floods supply, distribution gap is the opportunity. Today 1K apps → target 100K–1M. Network effects: more apps → more users → more creators. 17K Discord, 4K GitHub stars, open source community as engineering force.
