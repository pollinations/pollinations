# Pollinations.AI — Traction-Led Deck (8 slides)

## Slide 1: Cover + Traction

## Slide 2: Problem
Vibe coding era — anyone builds an AI app in an afternoon. But success punishes: viral app = thousands in compute bills. Platforms charge the developer. Users won't subscribe to micro-apps. Creators go broke before earning a dollar.

## Slide 3: Product
Show the product. One API, 38+ models, 14 providers. Zero backend, zero payment setup. Unified Pollen wallet across all apps (1 Pollen = $1). Creator builds, user pays per use, creator earns. Include a product screenshot or demo flow.

## Slide 4: Why Now + Market
Three forces: model costs collapsing, vibe coding creating millions of non-dev creators, value shifting from models to apps. ~50M potential creators (app stores, Roblox, vibe coders). Today 1K apps/3M users → target 100K+ apps. Benchmarks: Roblox 40M experiences, YouTube 50M creators.

## Slide 5: Business Model + Unit Economics
Users buy Pollen, spend across apps. We take a cut of every transaction. Flywheel: more apps → more Pollen demand → more creator revenue → more creators. ~$6K MRR today at cost. Revenue share and margin activate Q2 2026. Proof point: one Roblox game — 51M visits, $12K/month user spend.

## Slide 6: Go-to-Market + Competition
GTM is the product itself: free API credits as acquisition cost, every app is a distribution channel, open source means apps fork and multiply. Vs. competitors: HuggingFace/Replicate host models, OpenRouter/Vercel sell to devs — we're the layer above, earning from end users not developers. Two-sided network + community engineering as moats.
