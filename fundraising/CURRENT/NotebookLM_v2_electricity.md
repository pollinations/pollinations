# Pollinations.AI — Electricity Analogy Deck (8 slides)

AI compute as utility. Creators are businesses. Users pay the electric bill.

## Slide 1: Cover + Analogy
"The Creator Economy for AI Experiences." AI apps run on compute like shops run on electricity. We're the power company — creators plug in, users pay the meter, everyone wins.

## Slide 2: The Current Mess
For creators: imagine opening a shop where YOU pay the customer's electric bill. That's every AI platform today — so creators never turn the lights on. They self-limit, cap users, skip features, never promote. The best apps never get built because creators can't risk popularity. For users: imagine paying a separate electric company for every appliance in your house. That's subscription hell — 50 AI apps, 50 monthly fees.

## Slide 3: One Meter, Every App
Pollen (1 = $1) — one prepaid balance that works across all AI apps. Users pay for what they use, like metered electricity. No subscriptions, no waste. Creators earn per transaction with zero payment infrastructure. The user pays the meter, the creator gets the revenue, we keep the grid running.

## Slide 4: The Grid
Phase 1 — Power Plant (DONE): One API, 38+ models, 14 providers. The raw compute. Phase 2 — Meters & Billing (NOW): Pollen payments, creator rev-share. Revenue flows. Phase 3 — Service Directory (NEXT): Marketplace and discovery. How users find which apps to power up.

## Slide 5: The Grid Is Live
500+ apps connected, 3M users on the grid, 500+ new creators/day, 2.4M daily calls. All organic, $0 marketing. One Roblox app: 51M visits, $12K/month flowing through the meter. ~$6K MRR at cost. Revenue share Q2 2026.

## Slide 6: Why Now + Scale
Models commoditizing — compute is becoming cheap utility. Vibe coding means ~50M potential creators will need to plug in somewhere. Nobody owns the utility layer for AI apps yet. Benchmarks: Roblox 40M experiences, YouTube 50M creators. We're at 1K apps — the grid is just warming up.
