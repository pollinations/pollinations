# Story & Vision Revised V2: Open Source Exponential Growth
**Combining economic model with open source philosophy**

## Story Bullets (~60 sec)

### Opening Hook
- "We open-sourced everything. Expected copycats. Got contributors instead."

### The Experiment
- Inspired by GitLab, Buffer's transparency (but not "radical")
- Decision: Make code, pricing, roadmap public
- Mental model: Zero overhead deciding what to hide

### The Discovery
- 300+ developers now building WITH us
- Example contributions:
  - Minecraft integrations built while we slept
  - 7 Roblox games from community
  - Portrait XO voice clone (3 community members, 18-20 years old)
  - New AI models deployed by community before we knew

### The Economics Work
- Built the "user-pays" model - revolutionary like free-to-play gaming
- Developers create → Users pay their own usage → We handle infrastructure
- Proof of work system: GitHub activity earns Pollen credits
- No more "build app → go viral → go broke"

### Validation
- $820 weekly revenue → 229% WoW growth
- 10.9B tokens/week processed
- Fork-to-contribution ratio: 1:3 (industry avg 1:20)
- 600+ new developers daily choosing open platform

## Vision Bullets (~30 sec)

### The Philosophy
- Open source creates compound effects
- Community contributions > corporate R&D
- Every line of code makes platform stronger
- We're building infrastructure others can trust

### Business Model Evolution
- Today: Pollen credits (working)
- Tomorrow: Revenue share with builders
- Future: White-label for enterprises
- Model proven: Red Hat ($34B exit), Databricks ($134B valuation)

### The Unfair Advantage
- Can't buy 17K community helping each other
- Zero marketing: Community creates tutorials, spreads word
- Zero R&D waste: Community validates every feature
- Already in AI training data = permanent organic discovery

### The Outcome
- Enabling the creator economy (68% of Gen Z earning digitally)
- Standard infrastructure for AI apps
- Taking small percentage of massive market
- "Like app stores, but open - we process transactions, not control them"
